Hi I'm Zye.
Suneung(Korean SAT) math exams are quite infamous for being difficult, but I've rarely seen them translated in English.
So I've done it myself! You can now have the full Suneung math experience!
Below I have the 2019 Suneung Math (Type Ga) translated in English, which is the exam I took myself for college entrance.

Info

  • Time: 100 minutes
  • Total score: 100 points
  • 30 questions
    • 21 Multiple choice questions
    • 9 Short answer questions
The answer to each short answer question is an integer from 1 to 999.
Calculators are NOT allowed.

Stats

This exam was taken by 168,512 students.
  • 655 students(0.39%) got a score of 100.
  • 10,803 students(6.41%) got a score of 90 or more.
  • 48,076 students(28.5%) got a score of 80 or more.
  • 77,339 students(45.9%) got a score of 70 or more.
  • 185 students got a score of 0.
Compared to other Suneung math exams at the time, slightly more students got high scores in this exam. So this year's Suneung math was slightly easier than usual.

How do I take the exam?

Get The Exam

  • Try the exam above for 100 minutes. No calculators!
  • Send your answers to the "Zye's Korean SAT" thread on the #math channel in Math Club.
  • Afterwards, you can freely talk about the exam in that thread.
  • You can try solving the exam without a time limit too. In that case please tell that to me when sending your answers!

Tips

  • It's supposed to be hard without specifically practicing for it, so don't fret on getting a low score. If you get 80 or more it would be actually insane. I will be your tour guide if you ever come to Seoul
  • Try the questions in ascending order, and if you get stuck, try skipping to question 22. That's where the "short answer" section starts.
  • Answers to all "short answer" questions are integers from 1 to 999.
  • Math stuff:
    • \(_a \mathrm{P} _b\:\): Permutation. Same as \(\dfrac{a!}{(a-b)!}\).
    • \(_a \mathrm{C} _b\:\): Combination. Same as \(\dfrac{a!}{b!(a-b)!}\).
    • \(\mathrm{E}(X)\:\): Mean of random variable \(X\).
    • \(\mathrm{V}(X)\:\): Variance of random variable \(X\).
  • I will give you a +1 point bonus if you write the phrase "Never have I seen one as lovely as you" in your own handwriting and send it along the answers. Trust me, this is important.



Challenge Question!

This is the hardest problem in Korean SAT math history in my opinion. It also has a very beautiful solution.
It is from a different exam than the one above, so you can try it independently.
Try solving it with no calculators!
Better if you can write out your reasoning.
First person to solve Everyone who solves in the math club gets enormous fame and fortune.

See the problem
A function \(f(x)\) defined on \(x>a\), and a quartic function \(g(x)\) with a leading coefficient of \(-1\), satisfy the following. (※ \(a\) is a constant.)
  1. \((x-a)f(x)=g(x)\) for all \(x>a\).
  2. For some real numbers \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) (\(\alpha \ne \beta\)),
    the function \(f(x)\) has a local maximum of \(M\) at both \(x=\alpha\) and \(x=\beta\).
    (※ \(M>0\))
  3. The number of values of \(x\) where \(f(x)\) has a local extremum, is greater than
    the number of values of \(x\) where \(g(x)\) has a local extremum.
Given that \(\beta-\alpha=6\sqrt{3}\), compute the smallest possible value of \(M\). [4 points]
